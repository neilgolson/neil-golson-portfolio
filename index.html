Things I've built

Austin is the live music capital of the world. When I'm not working I'm finding live music — and I've been building AI tools about it. These aren't side projects; they're how I stay close to the thing I care about most: why people choose what they choose, and what it takes to earn that.

Live at panic.neilgolson.com

Panic Predictor

Widespread Panic has played more than 1,500 shows. Every night the setlist is different — shaped by rotation patterns, venue history, run length, and how recently each song last appeared. I built a prediction engine that ingests all of it.

The algorithm weights recency, historical play frequency, and set position to surface the top 10 most likely songs for any upcoming show. Currently loaded with 111 shows of data and predicting Jazz Fest. The name is a pun. I'm not sorry.

Next step: a live music and jamband prediction market — where fans stake real conviction on what they think will get played.

HeardIt

I've been to 255 concerts — Widespread Panic 150 times, My Morning Jacket 11, Phish 10, across 73 venues and 63 artists. For a long time that history lived only in memory and a few spreadsheets.

HeardIt is a concert history and setlist social network for people who take live music seriously. You log shows, track setlists, surface what happened on this day across your entire concert history, and share it with the people who were there. The social layer rewards being in the room — something streaming will never replicate.
